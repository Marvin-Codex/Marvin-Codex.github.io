Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

We recommend booking at least 3-6 months in advance, especially for gorilla trekking permits which are limited and in high demand. Peak seasons (June-September and December-February) require even earlier booking.
Our packages typically include accommodation, all meals, transportation, professional guides, park fees, and activities as specified in each itinerary. International flights are usually not included unless specifically mentioned.
Yellow fever vaccination is required for entry into Uganda, Rwanda, Tanzania, and Kenya. We also recommend vaccinations for hepatitis A&B, typhoid, and malaria prophylaxis. Consult your doctor 4-6 weeks before travel.
Pack neutral-colored clothing, comfortable walking shoes, sun protection, insect repellent, binoculars, camera with extra batteries, and any personal medications. We provide a detailed packing list upon booking.
The dry seasons (June-September and December-February) are ideal for wildlife viewing and gorilla trekking. The wet seasons offer lush landscapes, fewer crowds, and better rates, though some roads may be challenging.
Gorilla trekking can be moderately to highly demanding, involving hiking through dense forest terrain for 2-8 hours. A reasonable level of fitness is required. Porters are available to assist with carrying bags and providing support.
Yes, we can accommodate most dietary requirements including vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and other special diets. Please inform us of any dietary restrictions when booking so we can make appropriate arrangements.
Cancellation policies vary by tour and timing. Generally, cancellations made 90+ days before departure receive full refund minus processing fees. Closer to departure, fees increase. We strongly recommend travel insurance to protect your investment.
